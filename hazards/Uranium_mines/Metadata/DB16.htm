Attribute:

Attribute_Label: GIS_ID

Attribute_Definition: This 16 character field is the link between the GIS and the mine location. It is a required field and must be unique throughout the database. It has a specific format of “LMMDDHHLYYYYLLLN”.  The first “L” is a letter corresponding to the GPS unit used to collect the spatial data. “MM” is the digit month, “DD” is the digit day of the month, “HH” is the 24hr UTC clock hour. The second “L” is a character A-Z designating a unique file for that hour. “YYYY” is the for digit year of the file. “LLLN” is the user id of the gps person.  This identifier can be looked up the amludb users table. In the instances where GPS data isn’t available for a record (i.e. Forest Service data) but a latitude and longitude is known, this field may be left blank (Null) and the field ddlat and ddlon should be populated. A new unique gis id will automatically be created under these conditions. Note that if the gis id is left blank and the ddlat and ddlon field are not populated, you will not be able to save the record.

Attribute:

Attribute_Label: GIS_ACCURA

Attribute_Definition: The spatial accuracy of the position. There are five classes of spatial accuracy to choose from. The first, “15 ft.”, corresponds to differentially corrected GPS data. The second, “66 ft.”, corresponds to differentially corrected GPS data where an offset was involved.  The third, “328 ft.”, corresponds to uncorrected GPS data or positions derived from a topographic map. The fourth, “2700 ft.”, corresponds to positions derived from PLSS (Section, Township, Range) information. And the fifth, “Unknown”, is for those cases where no assessment can be made of the positional accuracy.
